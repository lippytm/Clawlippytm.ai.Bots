# Bot Product Family

This document defines the product-family concept for `Clawlippytm.ai.Bots`.

The purpose is to turn bot ideas into a structured, scalable product line that can support business growth, service delivery, automation, and future white-label or premium offerings.

---

## Product Family Goal

Create a flexible bot ecosystem that can serve multiple needs without becoming a random collection of disconnected bots.

The product family should support:

- business intake
- customer guidance
- operational automation
- research and support workflows
- premium service delivery
- future partner or reseller models

---

## Core Bot Classes

### 1. Concierge Bot
Greets users, helps them choose the right path, and reduces confusion.

### 2. Intake Bot
Collects structured information and routes it to the right workflow.

### 3. Funding Bot
Supports funding-related education, readiness, and next-step routing.

### 4. Automation Bot
Helps users explore automation-related opportunities and service paths.

### 5. Research Bot
Helps gather information, summarize findings, and support decision-making.

### 6. Sales / Offer Bot
Explains offers, packages, and premium next steps more clearly.

### 7. Support Bot
Handles post-intake or post-purchase guidance and issue routing.

### 8. Builder Bot
Acts as a deeper systems or workflow assistant for advanced users or internal operators.

---

## Product-Line Principles

- each bot class should have a clearly defined job
- shared infrastructure should reduce duplication
- prompts, routing, and policies should be modular
- bots should be able to evolve from simple to advanced modes
- serious commercial bots and experimental bots should stay clearly separated

---

## Commercial Modes

This product family can support:

- direct customer-facing bots
- internal operator bots
- premium support bots
- white-label bot packages
- partner / affiliate bot flows
- vertical bots for special industries or use cases

---

## Architecture Guidance

The family should eventually separate:

- bot identity and role
- prompts and scripts
- routing rules
- escalation rules
- analytics and conversion tracking
- premium or gated features

---

## Best Practices

- design every bot around one clear primary outcome
- keep intake and routing structured rather than vague
- make escalation available for complex cases
- connect high-value bots to offers and workflows
- reuse shared prompt and policy assets where possible

---

## Rule of thumb

A strong bot family behaves like a product line, not a pile of chat experiments. Each bot should have a role, a pathway, a measurable purpose, and room to evolve.
